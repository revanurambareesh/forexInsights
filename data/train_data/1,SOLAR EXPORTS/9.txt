', Joseph Ralbovsky', Intern in EERE\u2019s International Team',  ', President Obama and India\u2019s Prime Minister Modi met this week',  and agreed to expand the partnership between the two countries in clean energy and climate change. These initiatives build on a strong foundation of existing bilateral cooperation, and reflect both countries\u2019 recognition of the tremendous economic opportunity afforded by supporting clean energy programs that facilitate access to clean energy and expand the market for renewable and energy efficiency technologies in India.', 
', India is a nation with an abundance of sunlight and an emerging market for solar energy technology. To facilitate further development of that market, the Energy Department\u2019s National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) and the India\u2019s National Institute of Solar Energy (NISE) have collaborated to enhance the quality and accuracy of India\u2019s solar resource maps.', 
', Ground stations measure solar irradiation at a specific location and point in time. Satellite data provides information on variations in solar radiation over a span of time. Used together, employing a modeling approach developed at University at Albany, State University of New York, the two types of data reduce uncertainty in solar resource estimates.', 
', NREL recently used data from five on-site solar radiation measurement stations in India to validate and improve the accuracy of satellite-based data. The enhanced solar resource information is available publicly, so developers can access static maps, hourly irradiance data, and data in a geographic information system format.', 
', Why is this important? Many developers in India want to use high-quality American made solar panels. The Export-Import Bank of the United States (Ex-Im Bank) provides concessional financing for overseas development projects that have at least 51 percent American-made components. So NREL\u2019s solar mapping work has facilitated approximately $350 million in exports of goods and services to India since 2011,', 
', Ashok Pasricha, a senior technical advisor at the Ex-Im Bank, highlighted the importance of the assessment in informing lending decisions for banks financing projects abroad. \u201cEx-Im Bank has found NREL\u2019s Indian solar maps and data to be extremely useful in estimating solar resources, which are then used in financial models to estimate potential revenues,\u201d said Pasricha. \u201cWe rely on the NREL solar maps.\u201d', 
', Next, NREL\u2019s researchers plan to incorporate data from additional ground stations in India to continue refining these solar resource maps and data sets.', 
', With its large, sun-filled territory and rapidly growing population, India is a ripe market for U.S. solar exports\u2014and is rapidly expanding its renewable energy supply.',
