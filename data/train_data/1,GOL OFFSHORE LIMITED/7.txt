', P.R. Sanjai', Joel Rebello', GOL Offshore is one of the largest offshore oilfield service providers in India, offering drilling and offshore support services to oil and gas companies for exploration and production activities.
', Mumbai:',  GOL Offshore Ltd, earlier known as Great Offshore Ltd, has been taken to court by some of its lenders over non-payment of loans, even as the company plans to sell some of its assets to meet repayment obligations.', 
', 
				   
				Two government-owned lenders\u2014Exim Bank of India and Punjab National Bank (PNB)\u2014have filed separate winding-up petitions in the Bombay high court seeking to liquidate the company\u2019s assets to recover loans, according to two people familiar with the development. Singapore-based DBS Bank Ltd has also filed a winding up petition against GOL Offshore on 23 December, according to the court\u2019s website.', 
', Both ', Exim Bank',  and ', PNB',  had loaned money to the company via their overseas operations and hence these loans qualify as external commercial borrowings.', 
', While the cases were filed in December, they are yet to come up for hearing in the high court, added the two people, who asked not to be identified.', 
', GOL Offshore, in an emailed statement, admitted that one domestic lender had filed a winding-up petition, which has not been yet admitted.', 
', The company added that foreign lenders had received permission from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) to recover their loans out of the sale of assets if necessary.', 
', GOL Offshore is one of the largest offshore oilfield service providers in India, offering drilling and offshore support services to oil and gas companies for exploration and production activities.', 
', The company was formed when the offshore division of ', Great Eastern Shipping Co. Ltd',  was spun off into a separate company in October 2006.', 
', \u201cThis company has also become a non-performing asset in the books of both Exim Bank and PNB and hence both banks have taken a call to knock on the doors of the court. It is expected to come up for hearing soon,\u201d said one of the persons cited above.', 
', Exim Bank did not reply to an email seeking comment. PNB executive director ', Ram Sangapure',  declined to comment, citing client confidentiality.', 
', Exim Bank and PNB are just two out of a clutch of lenders that together loaned more than ', Rs.', 2,000 crore to GOL Offshore, but not all lenders have approached the court.', 
', A group of lenders that has loaned money to the company locally have formed a joint lenders forum (JLF) led by ', Axis Bank ', Ltd', .', 
', The JLF has been formed under RBI\u2019s new norms with an intention to arrive at a corrective action plan to prevent the account from turning bad.', 
', RBI has made the formation of such forums mandatory when the aggregate exposure of banks is more than ', Rs.', 100 crore and interest repayments are overdue by more than 60 days. An account that is overdue by more than 90 days is classified as a non-performing asset (NPA).', 
', Axis Bank did not respond to an email seeking comment.', 
', DVB Bank SE',  of Germany and DBS Bank are among the foreign lenders to the company. Both refused to comment citing client confidentiality.', 
', While announcing its second quarter results for the current financial year, GOL Offshore said that in respect of loans, corporate guarantees and dues including instances where recovery proceedings have been initiated, the company is making all efforts for early settlement by taking various steps including: ', 1)',  more aggressive employment of its vessels and resources;',  2)',  disposal of some assets including operating assets; ', 3) ', settlement of significant current dues and restoration of initial repayment terms; ', 4)',  and entering into corrective action plan as approved by the JLF.', 
', \u201cThe management is very hopeful of arriving at the settlement before end of the year 2014-15. On this basis, the installments due beyond one year as per initial repayment terms are continued to be treated as long term dues and unrealised foreign exchange gains or losses are also accounted...The company is also able to earn reasonable profit margin by carrying on its business in the normal course,\u201d it said in a note on 14 November.', 
', In a note on 6 October 2014, rating firm ', Crisil Ltd',  said GOL Offshore has delayed serving its debt owing to weak liquidity while its short-term debt had increased considerably. It said GOL\u2019s cash flows will remain under stress because of continued delays in deployment of its 350-foot jack-up rig.', 
', A senior shipping consultant, who spoke on condition of anonymity said GOL Offshore will face potential issues from clients in the event of lenders seizing its vessels.', 
', 

', \u201cIn the past, some of the shipping companies had sold their assets in co-operation with lenders to avoid embarrassment. GOL Offshore needs to strike a balance between gainful deployment of assets and desperate initiatives of lenders to recoup the money. While arresting (vessels) will impact the cash flows for the company, the delay in recovering dues adds stress to banks\u2019 books,\u201d he said.', 
', The banking industry has seen a build-up of stressed assets which has pushed banks to become more aggressive in recovering overdue loans. According to RBI\u2019s Financial Stability Report, released on 29 December, stressed advances, or the sum of gross NPAs and restructured loans, increased to 10.7% of total advances in September from 10% in March last year.',
