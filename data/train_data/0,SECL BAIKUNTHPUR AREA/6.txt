As a result, water supply from the dam has been discontinued, and now, the same is being supplied from Gej reservoir, on the other side of district headquarters. ',  Jhunka reservoir was built on the initiative of the then irrigation minister of undivided Madhya Pradesh Ramchandra Singh Deo. Construction of the dam began in 1977 and it was completed in 1994. Since then, the dam supplied water for irrigation to Baikunthpur and more than a dozen surrounding villages. Besides, it also supplied drinking water to the township as well as colliery township of Churcha, about 10km from here. ',  Untreated water from Chrucha colliery as well as Churcha township kept being discharged into the dam through Gagar Gudi nullah, which originates from Chrucha hills and flows into the dam. This practice went on for years unchecked, until it was too late. ',  A filtration plant was set up at Churcha colliery, but it remained a mere showpiece as it hardly functioned. Only recently, when a lot of hue and cry was raised with regard to Jhunka dam being polluted beyond permissible limits, the colliery authorities took an initiative and got the filtration plant to function a bit more than it was functioning earlier. Sources claimed that the said plant has been lying nonfunctional for more than six years, SECL authorities concerned at Churcha, however, denied the allegation for obvious reasons. ', u" SECL GM Baikunthpur Kuldeep Prasad claimed that the filtration plant is working 'very nicely' and there was no issue with that. At times only surplus untreated water gets discharged into the Gagar Gudi nullah, otherwise no unttreated water is being discharged into the nullah, he added. On the other hand, speaking to TOI, Korea SDO (irrigation) SK Dubey said the effluent being discharged from Churcha colliery has polluted Jhunka dam water beyond permissible limits. As a result, water supply from the dam has been discontinued for over two years now. Moreover, the pollution level in dam water has also begun to adversely affect agricultural output, as well as fisheries production. Dubey further informed that fish seeds put in hatcheries in Jhunka dam resulted in little or no growth, while the same seeds when put in hatcheries in nearby Gej reservoir showed marked improvement. As a result, all the fish seeds from Jhunka reservoir are being dumped in Gej reservoir hatcheries. ",  Last year, district administration ordered a sample check of Gagar Gudi nullah that flows into Jhunka dam and the results (copies of which are with TOI) clearly indicated that the water had bacteria as well as pollutants way beyond permissible limits. ',  Locals, who used to go the dam for their bathing purposes have stopped the practice as many come up with complaints of itching and irritation.
