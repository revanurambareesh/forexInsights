', Bangalore: ', Adani Ports and Special Economic Zone Ltd (APSEZ) is eyeing port assets in Maharashtra, Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh, the three coastal states where billionaire Gautam Adani\u2019s ports business doesn\u2019t have a presence, a top company official said.', 
', 
				   
				\u201cThese are the three missing pieces in our own \u2018sagar mala\u2019 (an ambitious port project flagged off by the Union government),\u201d said Karan Adani, Gautam Adani\u2019s son and executive director at APSEZ. ', 
', APSEZ runs a string of ports along the western and eastern coast from Mundra, Hazira, Dahej, Mormugao, Visakhapatnam, Kamarajar, Kattupalli to the planned Vizhinjam facility.', 
', \u201cWe are actively scouting for greenfield sites and acquisitions. These are the three focus states for us,\u201d Karan said in an interview on 4 December.', 
', The Adani scion said that acquisition of ports had its problems such as high valuation. \u201cYou can\u2019t pay that kind of money. It is not sustainable. Our tendency is more towards greenfield. We bought Dhamra port in May 2014. Kattupalli, which we have acquired last month, is relatively small. If you look at the valuation also, it is more or less near the asset value; we are not paying any premium for that. If you look at any other ports, they would generally ask for a premium. It becomes a little bit unreasonable at that point. It is a matter of finding the right balance,\u201d said Karan. ', 
', He said that plans to acquire Gangavaram port in Andhra Pradesh fell through due to high valuations. \u201cWe looked at it, but the valuation was very high and it did not make sense for us. We are qualified for the Bhavanapadu port tender issued by the Andhra Pradesh government,\u201d he said. \u201cMargins are not that easy in ports, it\u2019s not easy to make money,\u201d he added. ', 
', The biggest advantage of a greenfield port is that one can manage cash flows. APSEZ, he said, has enough internal accruals to fund expansion plans. \u201cIf you go for acquisitions, that\u2019s when you have to roll out huge amounts, but if you look at green-field sites, you don\u2019t need to spend more than ', Rs.', 200 crore initially. Your investment is spread out over three-four years. We get almost ', Rs.', 2,500-3,000 crore cash every year. It is good enough to sustain our growth plans\u201d.', 
', APSEZ is also looking to expand overseas in such a way that it augments its ports in India. \u201cOur strategy is to look at Bay of Bengal, look at Bangladesh, Myanmar, South East Asia, Indonesia, Malaysia, Eastern Africa region where you can tie-up with your ports in India. For instance, we can make a transhipment hub in South East Asia and then give an option for shipping lines to use Vizhinjam as well. So, if lines want to go from China to Europe, they can use Vizhinjam, they can use the hub port in South East Asia. Our story has always been India-centric,\u201d he said.', 
', Karan said APSEZ would achieve the target of handling 200 million tonnes (mt) of cargo by 2020 at least two years earlier. The firm currently has a capacity to handle a combined 400 mt of cargo across all its facilities, while it handled 144 mt of cargo in the year ended March 2015. \u201cOur capacity will rise to 450 mt by 2016-17 and we hope to handle 170-180 mt,\u201d Karan said. ', 
', The expansion plans also include building a new terminal, the fifth one, at Mundra port, the company\u2019s flagship port in Gujarat, with a capacity to load 1.2 million standard containers. ', 
', \u201cThe new terminal will take the overall container loading capacity at Mundra to 6 million standard containers a year and help us overtake state-owned Jawaharlal Nehru port in terms of container handling capacity,\u201d Karan said.', 
', \u201cAs a group, we are taking a conscious call to de-risk our hedges and that\u2019s why we are focussing a lot on containers. If you see our last three announcements, those are purely on containers at Kamarajar, Kattupalli and Vzhinjam. We see a shift happening in India, container is going to be the growth market,\u201d he said.', 
', APSEZ is working on other sources of energy such as liquefied natural gas (LNG) and liquefied petroleum gas (LPG) to grow. \u201cWe are looking at adding more LNG and LPG terminals at our ports. LNG and LPG terminals are under construction at Mundra. We will build an LPG terminal at Dhamra as well as an LNG terminal at the same port. We have signed term sheets with Indian Oil Corp. Ltd and GAIL (India) Ltd. We will build the LNG terminal at Dhamra and offer capacity to these two entities,\u201d said Karan. ', P.
