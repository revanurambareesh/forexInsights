      The Government has used its earthquake powers to approve the Silverstream Estates development at Kaiapoi.
  ', 

  ', 

      The project had been conditionally approved by Waimakariri District Council commissioners in September.
  ', 

  ', 

      However, Earthquake Recovery Minister Gerry Brownlee announced yesterday the caveats would be lifted under the Canterbury Earthquake Recovery Act.
  ', 

  ', 

      Silverstream had previously required adjacent land to be developed before it could proceed.
  ', 

  ', 

      Brownlee said sections would be offered in different sizes, either undeveloped or with completed houses, to give people as many options as possible.
  ', 

  ', 

      "A significant number of the sections will accommodate smaller units designed for older people, and there is a small shopping centre planned that will provide for essential community needs."
  ', 

  ', u"

      Silverstream would be one of the largest developments available to red-zoned Kaiapoi residents. Part of it was on land exempted from Christchurch Airport's noise contour by the Government last month.
  ", 

  ', 

      Developer Fred Rahme said interest in the project was unprecedented. "We've had over 850 registrations of interests and we haven't even gone out there. It's crazy. I've never seen that in my lifetime.
  ', 

  ', 

      "Those who have registered and will register, they'll have the first right to purchase."
  ', 

  ', 

      An initial release of 550 would start becoming available in three to four weeks, with the types of sections on offer tailored to meet demand, Rahme said. The entire subdivision was 1180 sections, and the remainder would follow from April 2013. "It will all just flow on. All of the land has been approved."
  ', 

  ', 

      Silverstream has had a troubled history, as its developers fought to win approval.
  ', 

  ', 

      It stumbled at several consenting hurdles, culminating in the conditional approval by the independent commissioners in September.
  ', 

  ', 

      Since then, all authorities had worked well together to make the project happen, Rahme said. "All the players involved have put their agendas aside and really addressed the dire straits of the district and what it actually needs. The journey may have been a little rocky, but the destination we've reached is quite a good one."
