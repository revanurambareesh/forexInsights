            ', Songwon (Hall 6 Stand B07) isn\u2019t one of the most famous names at K 2016. But the small (800-employee) South Korean additives producer is looking to expand. And it has Europe in its sights.', 
', \u201cThe resin industry in Europe is increasingly weak because the plants are small and the plants are old. I don\u2019t think that [the importation of shale gas] is going to change the game,\u201d said Maurizio Butti, CEO and leader of industrial chemicals at Songwon Industrial Group, at a pre-K press conference in June, in Antwerp, Belgium.', 
', Butti hopes to change the game by bringing new plants to Europe. Songwon, which means \u201cpine tree field\u201d in Korean, was a local Korean player, before eyeing global expansion in 2006. It now has nine plants worldwide: three in Korea, two joint ventures plants in China, and plants in Germany, USA, India and UAE.', 
', In August, Songwon\u2019s second-quarter 2016 results announcement showed the financial growth it is enjoying. The group\u2019s sales were 179.1bn South Korean Won (\u20ac143m), 14% higher than in the second quarter of 2015. For the first half of the year, the group\u2019s sales increased by 15% to KRW 362.2bn (\u20ac288m) and it generated a gross profit of KRW 99.2bn (\u20ac79m) \u2013 77% higher than in the first six months of 2015.', 
', Songwon\u2019s current area of expertise is as a supplier of One Pack Systems (OPS), which are multi-component, tailor-made blends of additives in a pelletised form, sold under the Songnox brand.', 
', In Korea, Songwon is also big in PVC stabilisers but it\u2019s not a market it intends to exploit globally.', 
', \u201cWe\u2019re too late to be a global player in PVC stabilisers,\u201d said Butti. \u201cWe are big in our local market but we\u2019re not going to be a global player in this, so we\u2019re concentrating on other areas.\u201d', 
', Songwon has highlighted manufacturing increases in its product range, which includes antioxidants and UV stabilizers. It recently expanded its OPS manufacturing with a new facility in Abu Dhabi. With an initial capacity of 7,000 tpa and designed for future expansion, this latest addition has identical production technology as Songwon\u2019s two other OPS plants located in Greiz, Germany and Houston, USA.', 
', In China, its 8,000 tpa facility in Tangshan produces high quality thioester antioxidants for the Chinese market, as well as other regions. The second facility is in the final stages of construction and is dedicated to the production of Songnox antioxidant OPS. Located in the Qingdao area of Eastern China, it is due to be fully operational in the third quarter.', 
', A product the company has just launched is a solution for high heat stabilisation for filled PP-based automotive parts. Other stabilisers will be launched at the K fair.', 
', Songwon has created five new business units, three of which Butti would not name at this stage, but the two he can are: lube additives and electronic chemicals. Both are important given that Korea-based Hyundai-Kia is now the sixth biggest automotive firm (leapfrogging Fiat-Chrysler and Peugeot-Citroën) and, as Butti says, \u201cJapan has lost the leadership position to Korea in electronics. We are in the right position to take advantage of this.\u201d', 
', In recent years Songwon has sold phenolic oxidants to the fuel and lube additives industry. It has now decided to place more emphasis on this market, selling a range of products under the Songnox brand name.', 
', Its entry into the electronics market is part of a cooperation with German technology group Heraeus. Under the agreement, the specialty chemicals co-developed for the electronics and semiconductor market will be produced by Songwon and marketed by Heraeus along with its existing portfolio.', 
', Songwon\u2019s existing product portfolio includes polymer stabilisers, alkyl phenols and alkyl cresols, PVC stabilisers, tin intermediates, polyurethanes, SAP and flocculants.',
